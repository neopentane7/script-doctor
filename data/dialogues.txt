## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Subtext / Unspoken stakes (Drama)

DOCTOR
You can still withdraw consent. Right up until the moment we begin.

PATIENT
(Long pause.)
What if I don't want to? What if knowing what I'm afraid of is the only thing keeping me here?

DOCTOR
(Sets down her pen.)
That's not a medical question.

PATIENT
No. It isn't.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Moral conflict, no easy villain (Crime / Drama)

INVESTIGATOR
Nineteen witnesses. Nineteen people who saw what happened and said nothing. You were the twentieth.

SIMMONS
I had kids in the car.

INVESTIGATOR
The victim had kids at home.

SIMMONS
(After a long silence.)
I know. I think about them every day.

INVESTIGATOR
(Quietly.)
Then why are you only talking to me now?

SIMMONS
Because they're old enough to ask questions.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Power reversal, economy of words (Thriller)

MINISTER
You have nothing. You're bluffing.

CHEN
(Slides a single photograph across the table. Face down.)
I have everything. I'm giving you a choice.

(The Minister doesn't touch the photo.)

MINISTER
What do you want?

CHEN
What I've always wanted. For you to be afraid of the right things.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Intimacy used as weapon (Psychological Drama)

MARA
You used to call me at midnight just to read me weather reports from cities we'd never visit.

JULIAN
(Stiffens.)
That was a long time ago.

MARA
Four years. Three months. Eleven days. You've been counting too, or you would've said "years."

JULIAN
(After a beat.)
What do you want from me, Mara?

MARA
I want you to stop pretending you don't know exactly what this is.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — High-stakes revelation with no raised voices (Sci-Fi)

COMMANDER
You falsified the atmospheric readings.

SELENE
I corrected them.

COMMANDER
There's a colony of nine thousand people who think they can breathe down there.

SELENE
There's a colony of nine thousand people who would still be in cryo-sleep arguing about fuel ratios if I hadn't.

COMMANDER
That is not your call to make.

SELENE
(Turns to face the viewport.)
No. It was yours. You just didn't make it.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Comic timing through deflection (Comedy / Drama hybrid)

AUNT PEARL
Your cousin Fletcher got promoted. Regional manager. Twenty-six years old.

DANNY
Fletcher put a motivational poster over a safety exit. Someone could die.

AUNT PEARL
He's very driven.

DANNY
Mom —

AUNT PEARL
(Serves him more food.)
I'm just saying, a little drive never hurt anyone.

DANNY
Except the people who can't get out of the building.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Farewell scene, grief held at arm's length (Family Drama)

SON
You don't have to pretend it doesn't hurt.

FATHER
(Adjusting his son's collar — a thing he hasn't done in twenty years.)
I'm not pretending.

SON
Dad —

FATHER
I'm very good at this. I've had a lot of practice.

(He steps back. He is done adjusting the collar. His hands have nowhere to go.)

SON
(Softly.)
Me too.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Antagonist with a coherent worldview (Thriller / Action)

ROURKE
You think I'm the monster in this story.

AGENT
You killed eleven people.

ROURKE
I prevented a war that would have killed eleven thousand. The math is simple.

AGENT
That's not math. That's a story you tell yourself so you can sleep.

ROURKE
(Tilts his head, genuinely curious.)
And what story do you tell yourself?

AGENT
(After a long pause.)
I'm still working on it.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Romance, desire expressed through indirection (Romance / Drama)

PRIYA
You've been standing six inches further away from me all evening.

DANIEL
(Doesn't deny it.)
I'm aware.

PRIYA
Is that a new policy?

DANIEL
(Quietly.)
It's a precaution.

PRIYA
Against what?

DANIEL
Against saying something I'll mean. And then having to live with that.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Mentor / student, the moment the dynamic inverts (Drama)

PROFESSOR
I wrote the paper that established this entire field.

YUKI
I know. I've read it forty times. It's wrong.

PROFESSOR
(After a very long silence.)
In what way?

YUKI
(Opens her laptop. Turns it to face him.)
Every way that matters.

PROFESSOR
(Looks at the screen. Looks away.)
Show me.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Dread built from the ordinary (Horror)

BETH
The babysitter said she left at nine.

CARL
Then who turned the porch light off at eleven?

BETH
(Doesn't look at the window.)
Maybe it's on a timer.

CARL
We don't have a timer, Beth.

(Neither of them moves toward the hall.)

BETH
(Very quietly.)
Then let's not talk so loud.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Tension under fire, no wasted words (Action / War)

SERGEANT
On my go, you run. You do not stop for me.

PRIVATE
Sergeant —

SERGEANT
That's an order, not a conversation.

PRIVATE
(Grips his rifle.)
You gave the same order to Diaz.

SERGEANT
(A beat.)
And Diaz is why you get to argue with me. Go.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Guilt surfacing in a lie (Noir / Crime)

DETECTIVE
Walk me through it again. From the part you keep changing.

VERA
I told you everything.

DETECTIVE
You told me a version. Each one's a little kinder to you.

VERA
(Lights a cigarette. Her hand is steady — too steady.)
Maybe I'm just remembering it better.

DETECTIVE
People remember worse the closer they get to the truth. Not better.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Loyalty tested by ambition (Historical / Political)

QUEEN
You advised me to sign it.

CHANCELLOR
I advised you of the cost of not signing it. That is not the same thing.

QUEEN
Semantics, from the man who taught me the difference.

CHANCELLOR
(Bows his head slightly.)
Then let me teach you one more. A crown that never bends is not strong. It is brittle.

QUEEN
And the man who tells the queen she is brittle?

CHANCELLOR
Is either her most loyal servant or already dead. You'll decide which by morning.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Reunion where nothing is forgiven (Western)

CALLOWAY
Heard you were dead.

RED
Heard the same about you. Was hoping.

CALLOWAY
(Almost smiles.)
Ten years and that's your opening.

RED
Ten years, and you're standing on my porch with a badge you didn't earn.

CALLOWAY
Nobody earns these. You just outlive the man who wore it before you.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Wonder tipping into fear (Sci-Fi / Adventure)

NAV
The signal's not random. It's counting.

PILOT
Counting what?

NAV
Down.

(A long silence. The console pings — once.)

PILOT
(Steady, because someone has to be.)
Then we still have until zero. Tell me what we do with it.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Small kindness that breaks a wall (Coming-of-Age)

TEEN
You're not gonna tell my dad?

COACH
Tell him what? That his kid sat in the dugout after everyone left because home's louder than the field?

TEEN
(Says nothing.)

COACH
(Hands him a water bottle.)
I locked up late a lot at your age too. Field doesn't ask you how you're doing. That's the whole point of it.

## CRAFT EXAMPLE — Negotiation where the real deal is unspoken (Legal / Drama)

OPPOSING COUNSEL
My client will settle. Quietly.

ATTORNEY
Quiet costs extra.

OPPOSING COUNSEL
(Sliding a folder across.)
Everyone has a number.

ATTORNEY
(Doesn't open it.)
She doesn't want a number. She wants him to say it out loud, in a room, with a stenographer.

OPPOSING COUNSEL
That will never happen.

ATTORNEY
Then neither will quiet.
